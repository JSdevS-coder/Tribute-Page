MAHATMA GHANDI

Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi (b. Oct 2 1869 d. Jan 30 1948)

Gandhi was the preeminent leader of Indian nationalism in British-ruled India. Employing nonviolent civil disobedience, Gandhi led India to independence and inspired movements for civil rights and freedom across the world.

Most important moments of Ghandi's life:

  • 1869 - Gandhi was born in Porbandar, a coastal town in present-day Gujarat, India, Oct 2 1869.
  • 1883 - the 13-year old Mohandas was married to 14-year old Kasturbai Makhanji (her first name was usually shortened to "Kasturba") in an arranged child marriage, as was the custom in the region.
  • 1888 - Gandhi traveled to London, England, to study law at University College London and to train as a barrister.
  • 1891 - Gandhi Returns to India from London After Passing the Bar to find out that his mother died while he was away
  • 1893 - In April Gandhi Travels to South Africa to Work Under a Year-Long Contract with Dada Abdulla & Co., an Indian Firm
  • 1893 - In May Gandhi is Ejected from a South African Train, Motivating Him to Fight for Indian Rights in the British Colony
  • 1894 Mohandes Gandhi Founds the Natal Indian Congress, through this organization, he molded the Indian community of South Africa into a homogeneous political force
  • 1897 - Ghandi was nearly lynched in the streets Durban by a mob of Europeans who had been infuriated by Press reports of Gandhi’s advocacy of the Indian cause in his native land
  • 1899 - At the onset of the South African War, Gandhi argued that Indians must support the war effort in order to legitimize their claims to full citizenship, organizing a volunteer ambulance corps of 300 free Indians and 800 indentured labourers called the Indian Ambulance Corps, one of the few medical units to serve wounded black South Africans.
  • 1904 - Gandhi Founds the Phoenix Settlement. Influenced by the teachings of Ruskin, Gandhi purchased a farm at Phoenix in order to establish a communal settlement.
  • 1906 - Gandhi Introduces Non-Violent Protest Philosophy of Satyagraha
  • 1908 - Gandhi is Arrested for the First Time, for Refusing to Carry an Obligatory ID Card in South Africa
  • 1910 - Gandhi Establishes the Tolstoy Farm, which Serves as a Base of Operations for His Activities in South Africa
  • 1913 - Gandhi Begins "Great March" to Gain Indian Rights in South Africa
  • 1915 - Gandhi Receives Hero's Welcome Upon Returning to India from South Africa
  • 1917 - Gandhi Establishes Sabarmati Ashram
  • 1920 - The Gandhi Era of the Indian Independence Movement Begins with the Non-Cooperation Movement
  • 1921 - Gandhi is Given Exclusive Authority Over the Indian National Congress
  • 1922 - Gandhi is Arrested by the British Government on Charges of Inciting Violence at Chauri Chaura
  • 1924 - Gandhi is Released from Prison After Serving Only Two Years of a Six-Year Sentence Following Surgery for Appendicitis
  • 1924 - Gandhi Begins Twenty-One Day Fast in an Attempt to Reconcile Hindus and Muslims
  • 1930 - Declaration of the Independence of India
  • 1930 - Mahatma Gandhi Embarks on the Salt Satyagraha, The Salt Satyagraha was a campaign of nonviolent protest against the British salt tax in colonial India which began with the Salt March to Dandi
  • 1931 - Gandhi Sets Sail for England to Represent the Indian National Congress at the Second Round Table Conference
  • 1932 - Gandhi Begins Six-Day Fast to Protest Separate Elections for Untouchables
  • 1934 - Gandhi Announces Retirement from Politics to Focus on Village Economics
  • 1939 - Gandhi Announces Fast-Unto-Death in the Effort to Form a People's Council in Rajkot
  • 1942 - Gandhi is Arrested, Along with the Entire Congress Working Committee
  • 1944 - Kasturba Gandhi Dies at Age 74
  • 1946 - Direct Action Day, Also Known as the "Great Calcutta Killing" a day of widespread riot and manslaughter in the city of Calcutta (now known as Kolkata), Gandhi cancelled all his plans and decided to leave for the riot-affected areas
  • 1947 - United Kingdom Passes the Indian Independence Act 1947
  • 1948 - Mahatma Gandhi Announces Fast to End Hindu/Muslim Violence in Delhi
  • 1948 - Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi is Assassinated by Nathuram Godse
